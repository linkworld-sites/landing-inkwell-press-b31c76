There is a moment, unique to letterpress, that happens in the first few seconds after a pull. The press lifts. The paper peels away from the form. And for a beat — before you can read anything, before the eye has resolved the type — you simply see the impression. The shallow valley pressed into the cotton. The evidence of contact.

That moment is why we do this.

---

## How the studio came to be

*Pressed & Co.* did not begin as a business plan. It began with a 10×15 Chandler & Price platen press purchased from a retired printer in Bristol, loaded into a van, and driven through three counties in the rain. The press weighed six hundred pounds. The van sagged noticeably at the rear.

We spent two months cleaning the press before we pulled a single sheet. Ink dried in the rollers. A previous owner had painted parts of it with what appeared to be house paint. The chase clamps were rusted and required soaking overnight. None of this was glamorous. All of it was necessary.

> The best presses are always somewhat broken. They have to be made right, every time you use them. That is not a flaw. It is the craft demanding your attention.

---

## The paper question

If the press is the instrument, cotton paper is the score. We spent three months testing stocks before settling on our primary range: *Crane Lettra* in 110lb and 220lb, *Rives BFK* for art prints, and a small stock of Japanese tissue for envelope liners.

Cotton paper has a quality that woodpulp paper does not: *memory*. Press it and it holds the impression. Release it and the fibres spring back, but not entirely. The deboss persists. This is not a side-effect of letterpress printing — it is the point.

Your name, pressed into cotton paper, will look the same in twenty years as it does today.

## What we make

We print three categories of work:

- **Correspondence cards.** Single-colour or two-colour. Set in classic faces — Caslon, Garamond, Gill Sans for the bold — with your name, address, or monogram.
- **Wedding and event suites.** Fully bespoke. We work with a small number of clients each season, from initial design through to finished suite.
- **Art prints.** Limited editions. Our own designs, and occasional collaborations with printmakers and illustrators.

Each piece leaves the studio having been examined by hand.
